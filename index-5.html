3D Bioprinted hydroxyapatite loaded PCL scaffold

This work investigated the synthesis and characterization of hydroxyapatite microspheres (HAMS) and their application in drug delivery and bioprinting. HAMS were synthesized using hydrothermal techniques and characterized. The drug loading and release capabilities of the synthesized HAMS were evaluated. Further, HAMS were combined with polycaprolactone (PCL) to create a composite suitable for 3D bioprinting. The optimized composite was used to fabricate scaffolds using a BIOX6 3D bioprinter.

Acquainted with: 3D bioprinting, hydrothermal synthesis, ATR-FTIR, XRD, solvent casting, drug loading & release study

  • Internship
  • IIT Mandi

Chemical Profile and Antioxidant Properties of Propolis in relation to anticancer activity in leukaemia

Propolis is a natural adhesive produced by honeybees. This study explored two extracts, methanolic and ethanolic, prepared through Soxhlet extraction and maceration, respectively. The antioxidative, antibacterial, and anticancer properties of these extracts were assessed. For quantification, flavonoid, phenolic, and tannin contents were determined in both extracts. The antioxidant properties of the extracts were evaluated using DPPH and ABTS radical scavenging assays. Antibacterial susceptibility test of the extracts was performed to evaluate their efficacy against gram-positive and gram-negative bacteria.

Acquainted with: Soxhlet extraction, toxicity (BSLT), cytotoxicity (MTT), and antioxidant activity (DPPH, ABTS, SO, NO) assays, quantitative (TPC, TFC, TTC) and qualitative analysis, thin layer chromatography, docking (Autodock Vina)
