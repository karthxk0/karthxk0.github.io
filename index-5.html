Here, the model is applied to identify drugs to target metastatic dormancy and optimized for training on very small datasets.

3D Bioprinted hydroxyapatite loaded PCL scaffold

This work investigated the synthesis and characterization of hydroxyapatite microspheres (HAMS) and their application in drug delivery and bioprinting. HAMS were synthesized using hydrothermal techniques and characterized. The drug loading and release capabilities of the synthesized HAMS were evaluated. Further, HAMS were combined with polycaprolactone (PCL) to create a composite suitable for 3D bioprinting. The optimized composite was used to fabricate scaffolds using a BIOX6 3D bioprinter.

Acquainted with: 3D bioprinting, hydrothermal synthesis, ATR-FTIR, XRD, solvent casting, drug loading & release study
